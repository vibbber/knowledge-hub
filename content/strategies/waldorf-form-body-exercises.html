Form and Body Exercises

From "The Kingdom of Childhood" by Rudolf Steiner (1924), Lecture 4

Core Principle: Thinking developed through physical activity and spatial perception lasts a lifetime. Thinking developed "directly through the head" makes people "thought-tired" later. These exercises at age 7-8 create wisdom visible at ages 35-36.

Part 1: Form Completion Exercises

Show children incomplete figures and have them feel what's missing. The goal is to develop "observation permeated with thought, and thinking permeated with imaginative observation."

1 Simple Asymmetry

Draw a figure that is "unfinished" on one side. Ask: "Does this look complete? What's missing?"

Incomplete
Completed (child adds red)

Start with obvious asymmetries. Let attempts be clumsy at first.

2 Complex Symmetry

Progress to more complex figures where children must find the "inner logic" of the form.

Outer line given
Inner line added

Lines "close together" in one place, "separate" in another.

3 Curved to Straight Translation

Show a curved figure with its inner line. Then show the same figure made straight (with angles). Have children discover what the inner line should become.

This is difficult for 8-year-olds but "a wonderful achievement."

4 Reflection/Mirror

Draw a "water line" and an object above it. Have children draw what the reflection would look like below.

Leads to perception of "harmony found in the world."

Part 2: Body Coordination Exercises

Quick, alert exercises requiring children to think about their own bodies. Make them faster as skill develops.

Cross-Body Touch Exercises

Touch your right eye with your left hand!
Touch your right eye with your right hand!
Touch your left eye with your right hand!
Touch your left shoulder with your right hand from behind!
Touch your right shoulder with your left hand!
Touch your left ear with your right hand!
Touch the big toe of your right foot with your right hand!

Circle Exercises

Describe a circle with your right hand round your left!
Describe a circle with your left hand round your right!
Describe two circles cutting each other with both hands!
Describe two circles - one hand in one direction, other hand opposite direction!
Do it faster and faster!

Finger Exercises

Move the middle finger of your right hand very quickly!
Now the thumb!
Now the little finger!

Part 3: Color Harmony Exercises

Use paints. Show children how colors "belong together."

1 Complementary Discovery

Paint a red surface. Show that green next to it is "very harmonious."

2 Reversal Exercise

"Now I put green inside. What will you put round it?"

The child should discover: if red inside needs green outside, then green inside needs red outside.

Why This Matters
"Doing these exercises when children are eight years old will teach them how to think—to think for the rest of their lives."

Actions requiring alertness and forethought develop thinking more durably than intellectual exercises alone. "There will be a noticeable connection between the wisdom of such people in their thirty-fifth or thirty-sixth year and the exercises they did as a child of six or seven."
